PKKK/200/008 — Verifikasi Alat Radas (ISO 4787)

Gravimetric verification procedure for volumetric flasks, bulb pipettes, and burettes. Ensures volumetric accuracy using deionized water and temperature correction.

◈ Volumetric Flask Procedure
  1. Visual inspection: Check cleanliness, inner surface condition (no frosting), and inscription on the surface.
  2. Ensure the flask is clean and completely dry. Wear gloves before handling.
  3. Record 1 empty flask weight on the calibrated balance.
  4. Fill the flask with deionized water (room temperature) up to the meniscus mark.
  5. Record the water temperature before testing.
  6. Weigh the filled flask — record as reading 1.
  7. Empty, dry, and repeat for readings 2 and 3.
  8. Record the water temperature after testing — difference must be ≤ 0.5°C.
  9. Apply temperature correction using the UPM Temperature Correction Worksheet.
  10. Compare corrected volume against the flask's nominal volume and tolerance.
◈ Bulb Pipette Procedure
  1. Fill the bulb pipette with deionized water up to the meniscus mark.
  2. Allow gravity to drain the water from the pipette into a pre-weighed container.
  3. Touch and drag the tip of the bulb pipette against the container wall (do NOT blow out).
  4. Record the weight. Repeat for a total of 5 measurements.
  5. Apply temperature correction and compare against nominal volume tolerance.
◈ Burette Procedure
  1. Fill the burette with deionized water. Adjust the meniscus to the zero mark.
  2. Drain the desired test volume into a pre-weighed container.
  3. Record the weight. TARE and repeat for a total of 5 measurements.
  4. Apply temperature correction and compare against nominal volume tolerance.
  5. Test at multiple volume points as required (e.g., 10 mL, 25 mL, 50 mL).

◈ Corrective Action
  • ⚠️ If first verification fails → repeat verification by a different analyst
  • ❌ If second verification also fails → dispose of the glassware
  • 🏷️ Mark failed glassware as "OUT OF SERVICE" pending disposal
  • 📋 Document all failures in the verification record
