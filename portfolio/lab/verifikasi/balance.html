Analytical Balance Verification & Repeatability Test

Standard operating procedure for performance check of all analytical balances in the laboratory. Ensures weighing accuracy and traceability through calibrated standard weights.

◈ Procedure
  1. Check the balance is level and on a stable, vibration-free surface. Ensure the balance room temperature is within the acceptable range.
  2. Turn on the balance and allow at least 30 minutes warm-up time before use.
  3. Perform internal calibration (if available) or external calibration using the appropriate standard weight.
  4. Eccentricity Test: Place the standard weight at the centre, then at each of the four corners of the pan. Record all 5 readings.
  5. Repeatability Test: Place and remove the same standard weight 10 times consecutively. Record each reading. Calculate mean, standard deviation, and verify against specifications.
  6. Linearity Test: Weigh standard weights at 0%, 25%, 50%, 75%, and 100% of balance capacity. Record and compare against calibration certificate values.
  7. Record all results in the balance log book and repeatability test form.
  8. Submit completed forms to the Pharmacist for review and approval.

◈ Acceptance Criteria
  • ✅ Eccentricity: All 5 readings must be within ±0.5d of the reference value
  • ✅ Repeatability: Standard deviation ≤ stated repeatability specification
  • ✅ Linearity: Deviation from reference ≤ Maximum Permissible Error (MPE)
  • ⚠️ If FAIL: Remove from service, label "OUT OF SERVICE", arrange for calibration/repair
